# Approach Note

I started by reviewing the Nepal import reference guideline (NEPQA 2025) to understand what information is typically requested for grid-connected PV inverter reviews. I focused on the inverter section and used it as a reference for the type of information a Nepal import agent would likely look for.

I then manually reviewed the two manufacturer documents and compared them against those reference items. This helped identify key product details, manufacturer information, test evidence, certification information, and areas where the documents appeared to differ.

After the initial review, I organized the information into a structured process that extracted important facts from both documents, compared the information across sources, and highlighted missing or unclear items. The goal was not to make a compliance decision, but to create a review package that clearly shows what evidence is available and what still requires confirmation.

During the process, I refined the draft several times to improve readability. Early versions focused on collecting information, while later versions focused on separating the two document sources, highlighting inconsistencies, and presenting the findings in a format that is easier for a Nepal import review discussion.

The final result is a draft review package based on the information available in the supplied documents. Where information was missing, inconsistent, or uncertain, it has been noted rather than assumed.
